People are usually attracted to startup websites after seeing them online. This being said, one of the most important factors in the success of your project is building a good startup website.

The old idea that “first impressions are everything” is especially true in the case of startup businesses. Designing and hosting a website for your startup is critical because it reflects your values and who you are as a business owner.

Startup websites don’t have to be visual masterpieces. In fact, the best startup sites are often simple and straight to the point, with very few bells and whistles. There are, however, a few key factors that need to be remembered while designing your site.

A good startup will use minimal design elements but each one will be carefully thought out. Here, we’ve listed 38 of the best startup websites designs that you could get your inspiration from.

Ghost

Gigster

 

Like many of the other successful startups on our list, Gigster uses a minimalist design and simple features. They have all their content laid out in a manner that is easy to understand.

The heading of DocuSign is one of the best parts of the homepage. Not only does it lay out a few of the benefits right from the get-go, but it also sounds clever. Following it is a strong subheading and following that is proof.

There is also a short introductory video that tells visitors how the process works. The entire website uses basic navigation, which makes it simple to use on mobile browsers.

Ending thoughts on the best startup websites

Good startup websites don’t have to be elaborate in design to be effective and attractive. The above recommendations show that keeping designs simple is actually the best way to boost effectiveness.

Although the overall effectiveness of a website will depend on a few key details, none of them will make a difference if your site doesn’t get adequate traffic.
